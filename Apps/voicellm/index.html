Voice LLM Landscape

The three-layer picture every PM must hold in their head

The core shift: Voice AI is moving from pipeline models (STT → LLM → TTS, ~1.5s latency) to native multimodal models that hear and speak directly (<300ms). This changes product design, vendor strategy, and what "good" looks like.

Three Paradigms

Pipeline STT + LLM + TTS chained together. Flexible, composable, but adds 800ms–2s latency. Still dominant in production.
Native Audio Model ingests and emits raw audio (GPT-4o, Gemini Live). Sub-300ms round-trip. Captures emotion, tone, paralanguage.
On-Device Inference runs locally (Apple Intelligence, Qualcomm). Zero network latency, private by default. Constrained model size.

Why it matters for PMs

Latency Human conversation expects <500ms response. Every extra 100ms raises abandon rate in voice agents. Pipeline choice is a product decision.
Accuracy WER above 8% breaks trust. Domain-specific vocabulary (medical, legal, product names) needs custom vocabulary injection.
Naturalness MOS score drives NPS for voice products. Prosody, pacing, filler removal all affect perceived quality more than accuracy.
Cost Audio tokens cost 10-30x text tokens. Real-time streaming models have different pricing than batch. Model your cost per call minute early.

Product Use Cases

Voice Agents

AI phone callers for sales, customer support, healthcare scheduling, collections. Vapi, Retell, Bland power this category. Key metric: containment rate.

Realtime Assistants

ChatGPT Voice Mode, Gemini Live, Claude Voice. Conversational AI with natural back-and-forth. Key metric: conversation depth, barge-in handling.

Transcription + Intel

Meeting assistants (Otter, Fireflies, Grain), call analysis, compliance recording. Diarization + summarization. Key metric: speaker accuracy, summary quality.

Voice Cloning / TTS

ElevenLabs, Cartesia, PlayHT for branded voices, audiobooks, accessibility, game NPCs. Key metric: MOS, voice similarity score.

Language Learning

Speak app, Duolingo, Elsa Speak. Pronunciation scoring, real-time feedback. Needs phoneme-level accuracy and pronunciation scoring models.

Ambient / Edge

Always-on wake-word devices, smart home, wearables. Extremely low-latency, on-device VAD. Key metric: false accept / false reject rate.

Acronyms & Terms

The vocabulary that separates PMs who can lead voice teams from those who can't

Core Pipeline Acronyms

ASRAutomatic Speech Recognition — converts audio to text. Synonymous with STT. Evaluated with WER.
STTSpeech-to-Text — same as ASR. The "ears" of a voice pipeline. Key providers: Deepgram, Whisper, AssemblyAI.
TTSText-to-Speech — converts text to audio. The "mouth." Key providers: ElevenLabs, Cartesia, OpenAI TTS, Google WaveNet.
NLUNatural Language Understanding — extracting intent and entities from spoken text. Sits between ASR and action.
NLGNatural Language Generation — generating the response text before it's synthesized. Now dominated by LLMs.
VADVoice Activity Detection — determines when a person is speaking vs silence. Critical for turn-taking. Silero VAD, WebRTC VAD are common.
E2EEnd-to-End — model that handles the full pipeline (audio in → audio out) without discrete STT/TTS components. GPT-4o Realtime, Gemini Live.

Quality Metrics

WERWord Error Rate — (substitutions + deletions + insertions) / total words. Gold standard ASR quality metric. <5% is excellent, >15% is problematic.
CERCharacter Error Rate — same as WER but character-level. Better for agglutinative languages (Finnish, Turkish).
MOSMean Opinion Score — 1–5 human rating of voice naturalness/quality. MOS >4.0 is considered high quality. ElevenLabs targets 4.3+.
RTFReal-Time Factor — processing time / audio duration. RTF < 1.0 means faster than real-time. RTF < 0.3 is excellent for streaming.
DMOSDifferential MOS — MOS comparison against a reference. Used to measure degradation introduced by processing.

Latency Terms

TTFTTime to First Token — LLM latency from prompt to first output token. Target <200ms for voice.
TTFBTime to First Byte — server response start. In voice: time from end-of-speech to first audio byte returned.
E2E LatencyEnd-to-End Latency — total round-trip from user stops speaking to AI starts playing audio. Human comfort zone: <500ms. Pipeline systems: 800ms–2s.
JitterVariance in packet arrival time over a network. High jitter causes audio dropouts. Mitigated with playout buffers (adds 40–120ms).

Audio / Signal Terms

Sample RateAudio samples per second. 8kHz = telephony (narrow-band), 16kHz = standard ASR, 22kHz/44.1kHz = hi-fi TTS. Higher = better quality + larger files.
CodecAudio compression format. Opus (WebRTC, low latency), PCM (raw, lossless), MP3 (playback), AAC (Apple). Opus is the default for real-time voice.
PhonemeSmallest unit of sound in a language. English has ~44. TTS models synthesize at phoneme level. Used in pronunciation scoring apps.
ProsodyRhythm, stress, intonation, and pacing of speech. What makes voice sound natural vs robotic. Top differentiator between TTS providers.
DisfluencyNatural speech imperfections: "um", "uh", hesitations, false starts. Good ASR filters them; voice agents may inject them to sound more human.
DiarizationSpeaker Diarization — labeling who said what in a multi-speaker audio. "Speaker 1: ... Speaker 2: ..." Critical for meeting intelligence.

Native Multimodal

Model receives raw audio and emits raw audio. No separate STT/TTS step.

GPT-4o Realtime API — WebSocket streaming, supports interruption, ~320ms latency, $0.06/min audio in

Hume AI (EVI) — Empathic Voice Interface, reads and generates emotional tone in audio

Pros: lowest latency, captures emotion, one API call. Cons: more expensive, less control over each component.

Key Design Patterns

Barge-inUser interrupts AI mid-speech. Must stop TTS stream and process new input. Critical UX feature — missing it feels robotic.
Turn-takingDetecting when user finishes vs pauses. VAD + endpointing logic. Too aggressive = cuts user off; too passive = awkward silence.
StreamingLLM outputs tokens as they're generated; TTS converts each sentence fragment as it arrives. Reduces perceived latency significantly.
Backchanneling"Mm-hmm", "I see" — short acknowledgments AI emits while user is still speaking to signal active listening.

Trends

What's moving fast — as of mid-2025

2023

Whisper + GPT-4 pipeline goes mainstream

OpenAI releases Whisper open-source. Developers discover they can build voice products by chaining Whisper + GPT-4 + TTS. Pipeline era begins. Latency: 2–3s.

2024 Q1

Deepgram Nova-2 & streaming TTS slash latency

STT drops to ~200ms. ElevenLabs Turbo, Cartesia debut streaming TTS. Optimized pipelines hit ~700ms E2E. Voice agents become commercially viable at scale.

2024 Q2

GPT-4o "Her moment" & native multimodal shock

OpenAI's demo of GPT-4o real-time voice causes industry reset. Sub-300ms latency, emotion detection, natural turn-taking. Entire pipeline layer threatened.

2024 Q3

Voice agents reach mass market

Vapi, Retell, Bland hit growth inflection. AI SDRs (sales development reps), AI support agents, AI healthcare schedulers in production at Fortune 500. Containment rates hit 70%+.

2024 Q4

Regulation & deepfake watermarking

FCC rules AI voices in robocalls require disclosure. EU AI Act voice provisions enter force. C2PA audio watermarking gains traction. Resemble Detect, ElevenLabs watermarking ship.

2025 H1

On-device voice & ambient computing

Apple Intelligence on-device STT/TTS. Qualcomm AI Hub for edge inference. Always-on wearables (Meta Ray-Ban, Humane AI Pin). Privacy + zero-latency combo drives new product categories.

2025 H2

Real-time translation becomes table stakes

Meta SeamlessStreaming, Google Translatotron, and native multimodal models enable near-real-time multilingual voice. Global voice agent deployment becomes feasible without localized models.

Macro Forces

  • Latency compression: every 6 months another 100ms is shaved off
  • Cost deflation: audio token prices falling ~40% per year
  • Voice cloning commoditization: 3-second cloning now free/cheap
  • Vertical AI agents: specialized voice models for healthcare, finance, legal
  • Emotional intelligence: voice that reads and matches user sentiment
  • Ambient / always-on: move away from explicit wake words
  • Regulatory pressure: disclosure laws, watermarking, consent frameworks

PM Strategic Watch

  • Pipeline vs native audio: revisit architecture choice every 6 months
  • Vendor lock-in: abstract TTS/STT behind interfaces — swap is likely
  • Audio token cost: model carefully before choosing native multimodal at scale
  • Barge-in & interruption UX: non-negotiable for production voice products
  • Custom vocabulary: branded terms, product names, domain jargon need injection
  • Deepfake risk: voice cloning of executives, customers — plan for it
  • Accessibility: voice-first is a genuine accessibility unlock — quantify this

PM Vocabulary

How to speak fluently in voice AI product and engineering conversations

Why this matters: Voice engineers and researchers use precise jargon. A PM who uses these terms correctly builds credibility fast and writes better specs. A PM who uses them wrong loses credibility even faster.

Conversation Mechanics

Barge-inUser interrupts the AI mid-utterance. The system must detect this (via VAD), stop playback, and process the new input. Your voice product must support this or it feels like a phone tree.
Turn-takingProtocol for who speaks when. Human conversation relies on prosodic cues (falling intonation = done speaking). AI must model this to avoid cutting off users or waiting forever.
EndpointingDecision: "has the user finished their turn?" False endpoint = cuts user off mid-thought. Late endpoint = awkward silence. Tunable threshold in most ASR systems.
Full-duplexBoth parties can speak simultaneously (like a real phone call). More natural; harder to build. Requires handling echo and concurrent VAD. GPT-4o Realtime is full-duplex.
Half-duplexOnly one party speaks at a time (walkie-talkie model). Simpler to build, unnatural for conversation. Avoids echo but breaks flow.
BackchannelingShort acknowledgment sounds ("mm-hmm", "I see") emitted by AI while user is still speaking. Makes AI feel present. Hard to implement without interrupting the flow.

Speech & Linguistics

ProsodyRhythm, stress, intonation, and pacing. The difference between "That's great" (sincere) and "That's great." (sarcastic) is prosody. Top differentiator between TTS providers.
PhonemeSmallest unit of sound. English has ~44. "cat" = /k/-/ae/-/t/. TTS models synthesize at phoneme level. Pronunciation scoring apps compare user phonemes to target.
Disfluency"Um", "uh", false starts, self-corrections. Natural in human speech. Good ASR filters or labels them. Some voice agents intentionally add them to sound human.
ParalanguageNon-verbal vocal elements: laughing, sighing, tone of voice. Native multimodal models (GPT-4o, Hume EVI) can detect and generate these. Pipeline models cannot.
UtteranceA unit of speech bounded by silence or turn boundaries. ASR systems process utterances. Transcripts are collections of utterances with timestamps.

Engineering Concepts

StreamingSending/receiving audio/tokens in real-time chunks rather than waiting for full completion. LLM streaming (token-by-token) fed directly to TTS cuts perceived latency by ~40%.
Jitter BufferSmall audio buffer that smooths out network timing variations. 40–120ms added latency but prevents audio gaps. Tunable in WebRTC.
Echo CancellationRemoving the AI's output audio from the microphone input, so the AI doesn't hear itself. AEC = Acoustic Echo Cancellation. Built into WebRTC but can fail with low-quality hardware.
Noise SuppressionFiltering background noise before STT. Critical for contact center use cases (open offices, call centers). Built into WebRTC; also offered by Krisp, NVIDIA RTX Voice.
ConcurrencyHow many simultaneous voice sessions a system handles. Relevant for capacity planning. Stateful WebSocket connections are more expensive than stateless HTTP.

Product & Safety Terms

Voice CloningCreating a synthetic voice from audio samples (as little as 3 seconds). Used for personalization; also primary deepfake risk. Requires explicit consent frameworks in product.
WatermarkingEmbedding invisible signal in AI-generated audio to identify its origin. ElevenLabs, Resemble, Adobe all ship this. C2PA is the emerging standard.
PII RedactionAuto-detecting and redacting names, SSNs, card numbers from transcripts. AssemblyAI and Deepgram offer this. Required for HIPAA and PCI compliance.
Custom VocabInjecting domain-specific words (product names, acronyms, proper nouns) into ASR model to reduce WER on specialized terms. Supported by Deepgram, AssemblyAI, Whisper via prompting.
HallucinationIn voice context: ASR inventing words not spoken (insertion errors) or LLM asserting false info in speech output. Separate failure modes requiring separate mitigations.

PM Checklist: Evaluating a Voice AI Vendor

  • WER on YOUR domain/vocabulary, not their benchmark
  • Streaming latency (TTFB / E2E), not batch performance
  • Barge-in / interruption support — demo it live
  • Language and accent coverage for your users
  • Custom vocabulary / hotword injection support
  • Diarization quality if you have multi-speaker audio
  • Cost per minute at your projected volume (get tier pricing)
  • SLA and uptime guarantees (>99.9% for production)
  • Data retention and privacy policy (SOC 2, HIPAA if needed)
  • Watermarking / consent tooling for voice cloning features
  • SDK quality and WebSocket reliability
  • Fallback / failover strategy for outages
